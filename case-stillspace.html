Why This Product Exists

Most mental health apps are built around productivity: streaks, goals, daily habits. They work well for people with moderate stress. But for someone in a depressive episode — when getting out of bed is a victory — these apps feel like another thing to fail at.

I wanted to create something different: a companion that meets you where you are, with zero pressure and zero judgment. Not a therapy replacement, but a quiet place to land when you need one.

The problem space

  • Depression affects 280M+ people globally (WHO, 2023) — yet most digital tools focus on prevention, not support during episodes
  • Burnout is rising — 77% of professionals report burnout at their current job (Deloitte, 2024), with emotional exhaustion as the #1 symptom
  • Existing apps create pressure — streak-based gamification punishes users for "bad days" instead of supporting them
  • Privacy concerns — 81% of mental health app users worry about data sharing (Mozilla Foundation, 2023); most apps fail basic privacy standards

Target audience

  • People living with depression, burnout, and emotional overload
  • Users experiencing apathy or severe fatigue — when even small tasks feel overwhelming
  • Anyone seeking mental health support without judgment, tracking, or performance pressure

Design Philosophy

Every design decision in StillSpace starts from one question: "Would this feel safe for someone on their worst day?"

Core principles

  • Warmth first: Warm palette (peach, lavender, soft grays), rounded shapes, zero visual aggression. The interface should feel like a hug — never push, never rush
  • Gentle clarity: One screen, one action. Minimal cognitive load. Copy is caring, not clinical — it acknowledges struggle without judgment
  • Quiet delight: Subtle micro-interactions — a heartbeat haptic in breathing mode, soft confetti after completing a self-care action. Celebrations feel earned and intimate
  • No toxic positivity: The app never says "stay positive" or "you've got this." Instead: "Just being is already huge work. You're doing it."

App Flow

The core experience adapts to how the user feels right now — not a fixed routine, but a responsive companion.

Mood check-in

Three energy levels — no complex questionnaires, no clinical scales. Just an honest, low-friction question: "How are you feeling right now?"

Adaptive actions

Based on energy level, the app suggests micro-activities — from "place your hand on your chest" (low energy) to "go outside, buy coffee" (more energy). ~150 tasks, randomized and never repeated in a session. No pressure — you can skip or stop anytime.

Just Be Together

For when you can't do anything — and that's okay. A breathing circle with gentle animation, heartbeat haptics, and ambient sound. No instructions, no timers. Just presence.

Journal & insights

A private diary with mood trends, completed action history, and pattern detection. No prompts, no requirements. Write when you want — or don't.

Crisis support

Verified helpline numbers always one tap away — contextualized by language (Ukrainian, English, Russian). Direct access to professional psychological support.

Building with AI

StillSpace was designed and developed with Claude (Anthropic) as a creative and engineering partner. This wasn't about replacing design thinking — it was about amplifying it.

What AI enabled

  • Rapid prototyping: From concept sketches to working SwiftUI code in hours, not weeks
  • Content creation at scale: ~150 localized self-care tasks across 3 energy levels and 3 languages, each carefully crafted for tone and clinical appropriateness
  • Design system as code: Color tokens, typography, spacing — defined once in design, immediately reflected in production code
  • Iteration speed: Testing ideas in real code instead of static mockups — seeing how animations feel, how copy reads in context, how flows connect

What stayed human

  • Product vision: The "why" — understanding what people in crisis actually need, not what apps typically offer
  • Emotional design: Tone of voice, visual warmth, the feeling of safety — these require empathy AI can support but not originate
  • Ethical decisions: Privacy-first architecture, crisis resource verification, no gamification of suffering
  • Curation: AI generates options; a designer decides what serves the user

Under the Hood

  • SwiftUI — native iOS, zero external dependencies
  • 100% offline-first — optional iCloud sync for backup
  • Zero data collection — no accounts, no analytics, no ads
  • 3 languages — Ukrainian, English, Russian
  • Accessibility — WCAG AA contrast, Dynamic Type, VoiceOver, minimum 44pt touch targets
  • Dark & light themes — warm palette in both modes
